University Archives document the broad history of the University of Idaho, and contain administrative records, university programs and activities, departmental histories, faculty publications and papers, student activities, UI publications, UI and private photographs of University people and events, and other records of University events, actions, and evidence of changing lifeways.

Below is a searchable and sortable table listing collections by title, group number, and resource links. The links point to a finding aid, which is a description of the collection and what it contains. In cases where that collection has been digitized, a link to a digital collection is also included.

You may also want to search the Argonaut Newspaper collections or Theses/Dissertation collections.

Each individual collection has a title and number, which are used to organize and reference the collection. Number prefixes show to which Collection group a particular archival, manuscript or photograph collection is assigned. For example, 10 stands for Manuscript Group 10, WILLIAM EDGAR BORAH, 1865-1940. MG signifies Manuscript Group, for Manuscript and Archival collections. UG signifies University Group, for University Archives records and documents. PG signifies Photograph Group, for Historic Photograph collections.

For further help, contact Special Collections via email at libspec@uidaho.edu or by phone at (208) 885-7951
